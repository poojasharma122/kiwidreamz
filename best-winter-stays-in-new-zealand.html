Best Winter Stays in New Zealand

The kind of places where staying in is half the point.

Winter in New Zealand brings snow to the mountains, mist to the valleys, and the perfect excuse to book a place with a fireplace or spa. Whether you’re chasing the slopes, soaking in thermal pools, or just looking for a quiet escape, these hotels make winter something to look forward to.

Matakauri Lodge – Queenstown

This luxury alpine retreat was made for winter. Suites come with open fireplaces, deep soaking tubs, and some of the best snow-capped views in the country. Queenstown’s ski fields are close, but it’s tempting to stay in

Why it’s perfect for winter: Private fireplaces, alpine silence, and total comfort just outside town.

Heritage Queenstown

Set on a hillside overlooking Lake Wakatipu, this hotel features alpine lodge architecture, an outdoor heated pool, and a roaring fire in the common lounge — perfect after a day on the slopes.

Why it’s perfect for winter: Rustic vibes, big views, and space for families or groups.

The George – Christchurch

Winter at The George is calm and classic — the park next door becomes frosty and quiet, and the rooms feel especially inviting. Pair it with a warm dinner at 50 Bistro and you’ve got the formula sorted.

Why it’s perfect for winter: Peace, service, and comfort in a city-centre boutique stay.

Black Swan Lakeside Boutique Hotel – Rotorua

Geothermal pools, lakeside spa tubs, and warm interiors set the tone here. Winter makes the steam rise a little higher from the water and adds even more atmosphere to an already peaceful place.

Why it’s perfect for winter: Natural warmth, lake views, and no need to venture far.

The Rees Hotel – Queenstown

Offering apartment-style living with fireplaces, heated bathrooms, and lakefront balconies, The Rees is a great base for ski trips and winter wine tasting.

Why it’s perfect for winter: Spacious suites, quiet setting, and a great on-site restaurant to come back to.

Millbrook Resort – Arrowtown

Snow-dusted fairways and mountain views make this resort ideal for winter relaxation. The spa here is excellent, and the stone-clad villas give a real alpine feel.

Why it’s perfect for winter: Village-style layout, warm interiors, and everything you need onsite.

Peppers Clearwater – Christchurch

Overlooking a still lake and golf course, this resort is calm and low-key in winter. Villas have fireplaces, kitchens, and enough space for cosy evenings indoors after cold days out.

Why it’s perfect for winter: Crisp, quiet air and self-contained comfort just outside the city.

Winter Booking Tips

  • Book early for ski season: Especially in Queenstown and Wanaka, winter fills fast.

  • Look for extras like spa access or late check-out: You’ll appreciate the slower mornings.

  • Pack for layering: Many boutique hotels stay warm indoors, so bring light layers and good outerwear.

  • Ask about fireplaces or heated floors: Many rooms feature these comforts, but not all advertise them clearly.

The cold brings clarity, and the right hotel can make it magical. These winter stays offer more than shelter — they turn the season into an experience.
